# Sensible Living Foundation Website Blueprint

## Purpose

The purpose of this document is to guide the improvement of the Sensible Living Foundation website so it becomes more user friendly, better organized, and more effective at turning visitors into supporters, donors, volunteers, and partners.

This blueprint is meant to help contributors understand the website goals and recommend improvements that align with the foundation mission.

## Foundation Mission

Sensible Living Foundation focuses on improving wealth and health outcomes for underserved communities through financial literacy education, healthy living education, and Sense Gardens that promote access to quality nutrition and community well-being.

## Website Goals

The website should help visitors:

1. Understand the foundation mission quickly
2. Learn about the main programs
3. Donate easily
4. Sign up for the newsletter
5. Volunteer or partner with the organization
6. Trust the organization through strong storytelling and clear impact

## Core Improvement Areas

### 1. Homepage Improvements

The homepage should:

- clearly explain the mission
- introduce the Wealth and Health pillars
- highlight the main programs
- include clear calls to action
- share impact and credibility
- guide people to donate, volunteer, subscribe, or partner

Recommended homepage sections:

1. Hero section with mission statement
2. Short overview of Wealth and Health pillars
3. Featured programs section
4. Why this work matters section
5. Impact or goals section
6. Ways to get involved
7. Newsletter signup
8. Footer with contact and social links

### 2. Program Pages

The website should clearly explain each major program.

Key program areas include:

- Financial literacy education
- Sense Gardens
- Future health and wellness initiatives

Each program page should answer:

- What is the program?
- Who does it help?
- Why does it matter?
- How can someone support or join?

### 3. Donation Funnel

The donation experience should be simple and trust-building.

Ideal donation journey:

1. Visitor learns about the mission
2. Visitor sees program impact
3. Visitor clicks donate
4. Visitor donates through Givebutter or donation system
5. Visitor receives thank-you message
6. Visitor is added to donor follow-up communications

Donation page should include:

- clear reason to give
- what donations support
- recurring donor option
- corporate sponsorship mention
- tax-deductible giving language

### 4. Newsletter and Email Capture

The site should encourage visitors to stay connected.

Newsletter flow:

1. Visitor sees signup form
2. Visitor enters name and email
3. Visitor receives confirmation or welcome email
4. Visitor is added to newsletter list
5. Visitor receives regular updates

The website should collect supporters through:

- homepage signup
- footer signup
- donation follow-up
- volunteer and partner forms

### 5. Volunteer Flow

The website should make volunteering easy.

Volunteer form should collect:

- full name
- email
- phone number
- skills or experience
- availability
- areas of interest

Volunteer categories may include:

- Sense Gardens
- financial literacy support
- events
- outreach
- operations
- partnerships

### 6. Corporate Partnership Flow

The website should have a pathway for businesses and organizations to support the mission.

Corporate partnership page should include:

- foundation mission overview
- overview of programs
- ways to support
- sponsorship opportunities
- employee volunteer opportunities
- contact form

### 7. SEO Strategy

The website should be easier to find on Google.

SEO improvements should include:

- clear page titles
- good meta descriptions
- better heading structure
- image alt text
- keyword targeting
- internal linking between pages

Keyword themes may include:

- financial literacy nonprofit
- community health nonprofit
- community garden nonprofit
- healthy living education
- nonprofit nutrition education
- underserved communities support

### 8. Storytelling and Trust

The website should build trust with visitors.

Ways to do this:

- share founder story
- share program vision
- include testimonials in the future
- show photos of work and community impact
- explain clearly how funds and support help

## User Types

The website should serve several kinds of visitors:

### Donors
People who want to support the mission financially

### Volunteers
People who want to give time or skills

### Community Members
People who may benefit from the programs

### Partners
Schools, nonprofits, businesses, and sponsors

## Success Measures

The website is improving if it leads to:

- more newsletter signups
- more volunteer inquiries
- more partnership inquiries
- more donations
- stronger engagement with program pages

## Phase 1 Priorities

Phase 1 should focus on:

1. Homepage clarity
2. Donation flow recommendations
3. Newsletter signup flow
4. Volunteer and partnership forms
5. SEO audit
6. Storytelling improvements

## Contributor Guidance

Contributors should use this blueprint when suggesting improvements.

Suggestions should be:

- practical
- mission aligned
- easy to understand
- focused on real user needs
