# Sensible Living Foundation Technology Stack and Tools

## Purpose

This document explains the current tools used by the Sensible Living Foundation website and outlines recommended tools for improving automation, engagement, and digital operations.

This helps contributors design improvements that work with the current systems.

## Current Website Platform

Website Platform:
Squarespace (or current site builder)

The website currently functions as a basic informational site with pages describing the foundation mission, programs, and donation options.

Current goals for improvement include:

• better user experience
• clearer program explanations
• stronger calls to action
• improved supporter engagement
• improved discoverability through search engines

## Donation Platform

Primary Donation Tool:
Givebutter

Givebutter is currently used to collect donations and manage fundraising campaigns.

Future improvements may include:

• improved donation messaging
• recurring donation encouragement
• donor follow up automation
• integration with email systems

## Email and Communication

Current communication methods include:

• direct email communication
• newsletter opportunities
• supporter outreach

Recommended improvements include:

• automated welcome emails
• monthly newsletter communication
• donor follow up messages
• volunteer follow up messages

Possible tools that may be evaluated:

• Mailchimp
• Brevo
• HubSpot for Nonprofits
• ConvertKit

## Forms and Data Collection

The website should collect information from visitors through forms such as:

• newsletter signup
• volunteer interest form
• partnership inquiries
• event participation

Information collected should help the foundation:

• stay connected with supporters
• recruit volunteers
• build partnerships
• communicate impact

## Analytics and Website Tracking

The website should track visitor behavior to better understand engagement.

Recommended tools:

• Google Analytics
• Google Search Console

These tools help measure:

• number of visitors
• popular pages
• donation activity
• traffic sources
• search performance

## SEO and Discoverability

Search engine optimization improvements should include:

• page titles
• meta descriptions
• keyword targeting
• internal links between pages
• optimized images

Key topic areas for search may include:

• financial literacy education
• community health programs
• urban gardens
• nonprofit nutrition education
• underserved community support

## Future Technology Opportunities

As the foundation grows, additional tools may be considered.

Examples include:

• donor relationship management systems
• volunteer management systems
• event management tools
• automated email marketing systems
• online education platforms

## Contributor Guidance

Contributors suggesting technology improvements should:

• recommend tools appropriate for nonprofit organizations
• prioritize affordability
• prioritize ease of use
• consider integration with existing systems
• avoid overly complex solutions

The goal is to build a digital system that is sustainable and easy for the foundation team to manage.

## Notes

This document may evolve as the organization adopts new tools and systems.
